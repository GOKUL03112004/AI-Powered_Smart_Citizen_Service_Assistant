BIRTH CERTIFICATE APPLICATION GUIDE

Overview:
A birth certificate is an official government document that records the birth of a child. It is a vital document required for school admissions, passport applications, voting registration, and various government schemes.

Who Can Apply:
- Parents or guardians of the newborn
- Hospital authorities (for institutional births)
- Any adult family member for delayed registration

Documents Required:
1. Hospital discharge summary or birth report
2. Identity proof of parents (Aadhaar Card, Voter ID, or Passport)
3. Address proof (Utility bill, Aadhaar, or Rental agreement)
4. Marriage certificate of parents (if applicable)
5. Affidavit for delayed registration (if more than 21 days after birth)

Application Process:
Step 1: Visit the nearest Municipal Corporation office or Gram Panchayat office
Step 2: Collect Form No. 1 (Birth Registration Form)
Step 3: Fill in all details including child's name, date of birth, place of birth, and parents' information
Step 4: Attach all required documents
Step 5: Submit the form within 21 days of birth to avoid penalty
Step 6: Receive acknowledgment receipt
Step 7: Collect the birth certificate within 7-10 working days

Online Application:
- Visit the official Civil Registration System portal
- Register with your mobile number and email
- Fill in the online birth registration form
- Upload scanned copies of documents
- Pay the nominal fee online
- Download the digitally signed certificate

Fees:
- Free registration within 21 days of birth
- Rs. 2/- for registration between 22-30 days
- Rs. 5/- for registration between 31 days to 1 year
- Court order required for registration after 1 year

Time Taken:
- Normal processing: 7-10 working days
- Tatkal service: 2-3 working days (additional fee applicable)

Important Notes:
- Birth must be registered within 21 days to avoid late fee
- The certificate is a lifelong document and should be preserved safely
- Corrections in the certificate require a separate application with supporting documents
- Digital certificates have the same legal validity as physical ones
