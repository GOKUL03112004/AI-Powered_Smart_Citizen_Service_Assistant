SENIOR CITIZEN PENSION SCHEME (INDIRA GANDHI NATIONAL OLD AGE PENSION SCHEME - IGNOAPS)

Overview:
The Senior Citizen Pension Scheme, officially known as the Indira Gandhi National Old Age Pension Scheme (IGNOAPS), is a centrally sponsored social security scheme under the National Social Assistance Programme (NSAP). It provides financial assistance to elderly citizens living below the poverty line.

Eligibility Criteria:
- Age: 60 years or above
- Must belong to a Below Poverty Line (BPL) household
- Must be a citizen of India
- Preferably have no regular source of income or very limited income
- Annual income should be below Rs. 2 lakh per year

Pension Amount:
- Age 60-79 years: Rs. 200/month (Central contribution) + State contribution (varies by state, typically Rs. 200-500)
- Age 80 years and above: Rs. 500/month (Central contribution) + State contribution
- Total pension typically ranges from Rs. 400 to Rs. 1000/month depending on state

Beneficiaries:
- Destitute men and women aged 60 years and above
- Widows of BPL families (covered under Indira Gandhi National Widow Pension Scheme)
- Disabled persons (covered under Indira Gandhi National Disability Pension Scheme)

Documents Required:
1. Age proof (Birth certificate, School leaving certificate, Aadhaar Card with date of birth)
2. BPL card or income certificate issued by competent authority
3. Identity proof (Aadhaar Card, Voter ID, Ration Card)
4. Address proof
5. Bank account details (passbook copy) - for direct benefit transfer
6. Passport-size photograph (2 copies)
7. Disability certificate (if applicable)

Application Process:
Step 1: Obtain application form from Gram Panchayat/Block office/Municipal office
Step 2: Fill the form with correct personal and financial details
Step 3: Attach all required documents
Step 4: Submit to the Gram Panchayat Pradhan/Block Development Officer/Ward Councilor
Step 5: The application is verified at block/municipality level
Step 6: Approved cases are forwarded to District Social Welfare Officer
Step 7: Pension is credited directly to bank account upon approval

Online Application:
- Visit NSAP portal: nsap.nic.in
- Select the pension scheme
- Fill personal details
- Upload documents
- Submit and track application

Payment Method:
- Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT) to bank account
- Post Office Savings Account
- Some states use doorstep delivery for rural beneficiaries

State-Level Additional Benefits:
Many states have their own pension schemes with higher amounts:
- Some states provide up to Rs. 2000-3000/month additional pension
- Free healthcare under state health schemes
- Subsidized ration under PDS (Public Distribution System)
- Free bus travel in many states

Important Notes:
- Pension is non-transferable
- Death of beneficiary must be reported to stop pension
- Change in income status (above BPL) should be reported
- Annual verification of beneficiary alive status is mandatory in most states

Helpline: 1800-111-555 (NSAP Helpline - Toll Free)
